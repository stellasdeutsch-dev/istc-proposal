The 60-day plan

Four phases. Fix first, then build, then grow.

Every task uses content ISTC already produces — plus the channels I already know how to run. No new budget: just a system, consistency, and hands that write the strategy and edit the video.

Month 1 — Fix & Foundation Month 2 — Build & Amplify
Weeks 1–2
Audit & repair

Stop the leaks and get instruments in place, so everything after this is measurable.

1Deliver the full digital audit — already done. A confidential findings note lands on day one, with the critical fixes ranked.Search-visibility & metadata repairs handed to the web team.
2Platform hygiene sweep — correct the Facebook location, unify profile data, and finally link the YouTube channel everywhere.
3Install real analytics (GA4) and publish the first baseline report — the number we grow from.
4Build the content calendar and a "48-hour event cycle" so no seminar or board session goes uncovered again.
Weeks 3–4
The content engine

Turn a warehouse of unseen footage and press releases into a steady, on-brand feed.

1Revive the YouTube channel — playlists, SEO titles, thumbnails for the 26 CBRN interviews and Expo series already sitting there.
2Cut vertical clips with subtitles from existing footage — a half-year of Reels, Shorts and TikToks without a single new shoot.
3Launch 6 content pillars, posting 3×/week on LinkedIn and mirroring to Facebook with a human tone.
4Ship the "ISTC at Expo 2025 — Results" page, replacing the one that still invites visitors to a pavilion that closed months ago.
Weeks 5–6
New reach — my home turf

Open the platforms ISTC's real audience uses — the exact ones I've already grown to 145k.

1Launch a Telegram channel for grants, jobs and event alerts — bilingual EN/RU, the region's default channel. I run one with 9,800 subscribers.
2Start TikTok & Threads — not dances; short explainers on biosafety, water security and what a science diplomat actually does. Where the next generation of scientists is.
3Roll out bilingual content so a Kazakh, Tajik or Armenian researcher meets ISTC in a language they read daily.
4Tag partners systematically (UNODA, ICGEB, EU delegations) so every post borrows a second audience.
Weeks 7–8
Systems that last

Leave behind infrastructure that keeps working after the internship ends.

1Assemble a press kit & "ISTC in numbers" fact sheet — the impact figures donors currently can't find anywhere.
2Ship a monthly email digest with a real subscribe form — the newsletter the site has never had.
3Build the 83rd Governing Board landing page (Armenia, October 2026) and a countdown for the Japan–Central Asia call.
4Deliver a before/after metrics report across every channel — proof the two months paid off.
