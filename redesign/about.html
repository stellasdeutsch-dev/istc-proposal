What ISTC is

Who we are

The International Science and Technology Center was established in 1992 to engage scientists from the former Soviet Union in peaceful, civilian research after the Cold War. What began as a nonproliferation effort has grown into a hub for international scientific cooperation.

Today ISTC facilitates projects in biosafety, CBRN risk mitigation, water and energy security, and science diplomacy — connecting researchers in its member states with partners around the world.

Our story

A network built to last.

1992

Founded in Moscow

Established by international agreement to engage former weapons scientists in peaceful civilian research after the Cold War.

2015

A new home in Astana

ISTC relocated to Nazarbayev University in Astana, Kazakhstan — reaffirming its mission at the heart of Central Asia.

Today

Ten parties, one mission

ISTC connects scientists across ten member parties with partners in nearly 60 countries, expanding into new fields like cybersecurity and clean energy.

Our mission

Creating peaceful, multilateral science & technology collaboration for a safer, more secure, and sustainable world.
